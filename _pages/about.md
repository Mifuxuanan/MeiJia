I'm a master's graduate from the Computational and Corpus Linguistics programme at the University of Manchester. I got my BA degree in Chinese Language & Literature from Shanghai University. My research interest lies in the neuro-symbolic AI and interpretability of large language models (LLMs), rooted in my Master's dissertation, where I explored both theoretical (formal methods) and empirical (intermediate representation visualisations) interpretations of Transformer-based models on first-order logic (FOL). I am deeply fascinated by the internal mechanisms of LLMs and their potential alignments with the human brain in formal and functional linguistic competences. I also want to figure out whether LLMs have genuine reasoning ability or merely rely on memorisation.

I have always been driven by a pure passion for explaining the latent complexities inside LLMs. With this foundation of curiosity, I am committed to becoming a leading researcher in Computational Linguistics, not only to advance the field of LLM interpretability, but also to contribute to the visibility and influence of women in the scientific community.
